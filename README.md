🛒 Consumer Shopping Trends: Behavioral & Demographic Segment Analysis
📌 Project Overview
Who is the most valuable consumer? This project analyzes shopping behaviors and demographic profiles of ~11.8K consumers across different city tiers. The analysis focuses on understanding how age correlates with income, spending habits, social media usage, and impulse buying. The ultimate goal is to identify high-value customer segments and propose targeted marketing strategies based on data-driven insights.

🛠️ The Analytical Stack
Tool: Power BI Desktop

Data Transformation: Power Query for dimensional modeling and data cleaning.

Visualization: Interactive dashboard featuring time-series analysis, sentiment breakdowns (Brand Loyalty), and demographic segmentation.

🔬 Methodology & Data Model
Segmentation: Consumers were grouped into four major age categories: Elderly, Youth, Young Adult, and Adult.

Metrics: Developed custom DAX measures to compare "Avg Store Spend" against "Avg Online Orders" across cohorts.

Filter Context: Dashboard includes interactive filters for Age Group, Gender, and City Tier to allow stakeholders to perform self-service exploratory analysis.

📊 Key Insights (Derived from Dashboard)
1. The High-Value Segment: The "Elderly"
Data disproves typical assumptions about age and technology.

Finding: While making up only ~20% of the population, the Elderly cohort earns the most (0.73bn monthly income) and spends the most (0.42bn avg store spend).

Impulse Buying: Surprisingly, the Elderly also showed the highest "Impulse Buying Score" ($0.00bn on chart represents minimal baseline compared to income).

2. Demographic Balance
The dataset features a remarkably balanced gender distribution (~3.9K for Male, Female, and Non-Specified), providing a reliable foundation for gender-neutral behavior analysis.

3. Surprising Social Media Patterns
The Elderly group also spends the most time on social media (13.89K hours), surpassing the Young Adult and Youth cohorts combined. This suggests that "traditional" media targeting may miss this high-spending demographic.

4. Consistent Behavior Across City Tiers
Analysis of Tier 1, 2, and 3 cities shows that consumer behavior (Brand Loyalty, Discount Sensitivity, and Product Availability) is evenly distributed at ~33% each. This indicates that marketing strategies do not need to be heavily localized; a standardized national approach could be effective.

💡 Strategic Recommendations
Prioritize the Elderly Cohort: Given their high disposable income and high-spend habits, they are the primary "high-value customer."

Digital Strategy: Realign social media ad spending. Focus digital marketing efforts for high-end or store-exclusive products on platforms favored by the 60+ demographic (e.g., Facebook, rather than TikTok).

Cross-Channel Opportunity: While the Elderly spend most in-store, their high social media usage indicates a strong opportunity for "web-to-store" conversion modeling.
